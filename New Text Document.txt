  <h2 class="welcome-to-greece"> welcome to greece </h2> 
  <p> Ancient sun-bleached ruins pierce blue skies as the Aegean laps at the endless coastline. And Greek culture is alive with passionate music, inspired cuisine and thrill-seeking activities.

  </p>

  <h2> The best tourist places </h2> 
  <p> <strong> <ol> <li> Cape Sounion </li> </strong> <br> 
    <p class="best-places"> Situated at the southernmost tip of the Attica peninsula, <em>Cape Sounion</em> is best known as the site of the ruins of the ancient Greek temple of Poseidon, the god of the sea. <br> 
      <br> <img id="1image" src="https://media1.picsearch.com/is?ODeEYT2KPgx4SFVTLAldtSaJxZXvd1UMRdlgTmMHOH8&height=256" width="300px"> </p>
    
    <li> <strong> Thessaloniki </li> </strong> <br>
        
        <p class="best-places"> Thessaloniki is Greece’s <strong> second-largest city and the capital city of the Macedonian</strong>  region of Northern Greece. Lively festivals, social events and a buzzing nightlife make this city the cultural capital of Greece.<br>  
         <br> <img id="2image" src="https://media1.picsearch.com/is?Fc6I6e1dc4JrmGidrwZYuI4QNoJzjZIKVnlmpx37K2s&height=226" width="300px">  </p> 
  <li> <strong> Halkidiki </li> </strong> <br> 
            <p class="best-places"> Halkidiki is a trident-like peninsula near the city of Thessaloniki, sporting excellent beaches. The three separate peninsulas can be roughly summarized as follows: Kassandra has the nightlife, Sithonia has the beaches and Athos has the monks. Being closest to Thessaloniki, Kassandra is more built-up, while the more quiet Sithonia has campgrounds, hidden coves and clear waters. Both are popular with Greek and Eastern European tourists. <br>
